Chapter 4: The Forest in Danger 🌧️

Daniel hurried into the forest, following the sound of the river. The trees swayed gently as if guiding him deeper inside. When he reached the glowing river, the Guardian of Nature slowly rose from the water once again.

Her light was dimmer than before.

“Something is wrong,” Daniel said.

The guardian nodded.
“Beyond the hills, people have begun cutting down forests and polluting rivers. The balance of nature is weakening. Even this forest can feel it.”

Daniel felt a heavy weight in his chest. He remembered the promise he made years ago.

“But what can I do?” he asked.

“You must do what you have always done,” the guardian replied. “Teach people. Remind them that nature is not just land and water—it is life itself.”

That night, Daniel returned to the village with a new mission. He spoke to the villagers, to travelers, to children in schools, and to farmers working the land. Slowly, people began to listen.

They planted trees.

They cleaned rivers.

They protected the forest.

And little by little, nature began to heal.
